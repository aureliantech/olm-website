The Method

The Counter Protocol

Instead of waiting for the board to build its case, we build ours first. Dismissal is not a hopeful outcome — it is the operational goal from day one.

01
Parallel Investigation

We build our case before they build theirs

From day one of retention, we independently gather exculpatory evidence, obtain and review all medical records, identify weaknesses in the complaint, and begin constructing the factual record that will anchor the rebuttal. We do not wait.

02
Evidence-Backed Rebuttal

The document where most cases are won or lost

A passive attorney files a form. We file a detailed, evidence-anchored rebuttal designed to accomplish one thing: get the complaint dismissed before it ever reaches a hearing. This is not a defense posture — it is a counter-offense.

03
Hearing Litigation, If Required

If the board won't dismiss, we litigate

If the board does not dismiss, we proceed to the hearing with the same rigor as criminal defense. Cross-examination of witnesses. Challenge of expert testimony. Being passive is not a strategy, and we don't treat it as one.
