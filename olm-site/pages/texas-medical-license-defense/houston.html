Why Houston Physicians Call Us

Houston's medical community is large, competitive, and deeply credential-dependent.

Houston is home to the Texas Medical Center — the world's largest medical complex — and a physician workforce operating across major health systems, academic centers, private practices, and ambulatory surgery centers. In this environment, a TMB complaint is not just a legal matter. It is a career-defining event.

Hospital credentialing in Houston's health systems — Houston Methodist, Memorial Hermann, HCA Houston Healthcare, UTHealth, and others — requires disclosure of pending board actions. A complaint that remains active triggers credentialing review across every affiliated institution simultaneously. The NPDB filing follows any disciplinary action and is visible to every future credential issuer, permanently.

The most dangerous period: the initial response window.

Houston physicians — often managing demanding schedules across large practices or academic centers — frequently underestimate the urgency of the initial TMB response deadline. The initial written response is the most consequential document in your defense. A passive response tells the board the case is uncontested. An evidence-anchored counter-response forces a dismissal decision. That window does not reopen.
