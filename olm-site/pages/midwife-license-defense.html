What Makes Midwifery Defense Different

Midwifery complaints carry unique vulnerabilities that require an attorney who understands the landscape.

Certified Nurse Midwives and Licensed Midwives operate in one of the most politically contested spaces in Texas healthcare. Board complaints against midwives frequently arise not from practice errors but from political and institutional pressure — complaints filed by competing interests, hospital systems, or advocacy groups seeking to restrict independent midwifery practice.

Your attorney must understand this landscape. A complaint framed as a clinical concern may be driven by an agenda that has nothing to do with the quality of your care. The Counter Protocol treats every complaint as a prosecution — and builds the counter-record accordingly.

CNM complaints also carry a longer average resolution timeline. If your case reaches a hearing, resolution averages two to three years. The financial and professional exposure during that period is significant. The only way to contain it is aggressive, early action.

What Your License Protects

The investment in your credential is real. So is the cost of losing it.

You invested 6–8 years of training and education to earn your credential. If you independently own your practice, your practice value is on the line. Your student debt does not disappear if your license is revoked.
