Octavia L. Martinez is a litigator, mother, and homesteader whose career is defined by a refusal to settle for the easy path.

Professional Background

A graduate of The University of Virginia (Dean's List) and The Ohio State University Moritz College of Law, Octavia began her career in Phoenix, Arizona — including an early associateship at Squire Sanders & Dempsey, one of the world's largest law firms. She gained rare judicial insight serving as a judicial clerk before transitioning to the front lines as a felony prosecutor at the Maricopa County Attorney's Office, Felony Criminal Trial Division.

She later moved into complex civil litigation at Renaud Cook Drury Mesaros, handling high-stakes matters ranging from commercial trucking defense to complex medical malpractice — learning how corporations and institutions construct their defenses and where those defenses are vulnerable.

The Move to Texas

Bringing this experience to the Rio Grande Valley, Octavia expanded her practice to meet the border region's unique needs. Her scope grew to cover defamation, specialized defense for medical providers — including physicians and licensed midwives — and complex estate matters where family, land, and legacy intersect.

Launch of the Firm

Octavia later paused her legal career to focus on stewardship: homeschooling her children and managing her family's homestead farm. However, she reactivated her license to assist a client whose legal matters had stagnated under conventional representation. That crisis highlighted the urgent need for uncompromised advocacy, leading to the launch of Octavia LaVon Martinez PLLC.

Today she has become a resource for clients who seek an alternative to the status quo — fully virtual, licensed in Texas and Arizona, and serving clients across both states in matters that require genuine legal firepower.
