When to Switch

Recognize the red flags.

These are the three most common patterns we see when clients come to us after being failed by prior counsel.

01
Ghosting
Silence from your attorney

If you cannot get a straight answer about the status of your case — or cannot get a response at all — this is not just poor service. It is a liability. Silence often means deadlines are being missed and the other side is moving while yours stands still. An attorney who does not communicate is an attorney who is not working your case.

02
Freezing
Passive when the case turns contested

Many general practitioners are comfortable filing paperwork but freeze when a case becomes contested. If opposing counsel has become aggressive and your attorney seems unwilling or unable to fight back, they may be out of their depth. A litigator who has never actually litigated is not prepared for what contested cases require.

03
The Local Conflict
More concerned with local relationships than your outcome

In tight-knit legal communities, some attorneys are hesitant to aggressively litigate against people they know — colleagues, politically connected figures, local institutions. If your lawyer seems more concerned with maintaining local relationships than winning your case, you need independent counsel. This firm has no such ties. We pursue every case on its merits alone.

Our Approach

We don't just pick up where they left off. We conduct a forensic audit.

Taking over a stalled case requires understanding exactly what went wrong — not just continuing from the current position. We go back to the beginning.

01
Stage One
File Review

We obtain the full client file from prior counsel. Every pleading, motion, correspondence, deadline, and financial record is reviewed to identify what was done, what was missed, and what errors can still be corrected.

02
Stage Two
Correction

Where the record allows, we file amended pleadings to fix mistakes, issue new discovery requests to surface evidence that was previously ignored, and take the corrective steps necessary to restore your legal position.

03
Stage Three
Acceleration

We end the delays. We set hearings, depose witnesses, and signal clearly to the opposition that your case is moving forward in earnest. The other side has often grown comfortable with your prior counsel's passivity. That changes immediately.
