What We Handle

Five types of workplace disputes — one firm.

This firm handles employment matters where the law was violated or an agreement exceeded what is legally enforceable. We are direct about what you have from the first conversation.

01
Wrongful Termination

Texas at-will employment has real exceptions. Termination that violates a contract, is based on illegal discrimination, or is retaliation for protected activity is actionable. We evaluate your specific facts honestly — and tell you directly what you have.

02
Workplace Discrimination & Harassment

Discrimination based on race, sex, age, disability, religion, or national origin under Title VII, the ADEA, the ADA, and Texas law. Hostile work environment and sexual harassment claims. We pursue what the law allows and do not overstate what we cannot prove.

03
Retaliation

Adverse action taken because you reported illegal activity, filed a complaint, participated in an investigation, or exercised a legally protected right. Retaliation is often easier to prove than the underlying claim — and carries its own separate damages.

04
Non-Compete & Non-Disclosure Review

Texas enforces non-competes only when they meet specific statutory requirements on scope, geography, and duration. Many are overbroad and unenforceable. Before you sign — or before you violate one — a review tells you exactly what you are and are not bound by.

05
Employment Contract Review & Negotiation

Before you sign an employment agreement, offer letter, severance agreement, or independent contractor arrangement — understand what you are agreeing to. We review, explain, and negotiate where there is room to negotiate.

06
Breach of Employment Contract

When an employer violates the terms of a written agreement — unpaid wages, violated restrictive covenants, improper termination of a for-cause contract. We hold parties accountable to their written obligations.

Our Approach

Assessment, pressure,
and resolution.

We are not interested in endless discovery battles. Our goal is a resolution that makes sense — financially and practically. That starts with an honest assessment of what you actually have before we recommend any course of action.

If the cost of litigation outweighs the potential recovery, we will tell you that in the first conversation. If the facts support aggressive action, we move immediately — drafting pleadings that signal to the opposition that we are prepared for trial, not just settlement.

Step One
Assessment

We analyze the economic and legal reality of your situation immediately. What you have, what it is worth, what it will take to pursue it, and whether pursuing it makes sense for you — all addressed in the first session.

Step Two
Pressure

We draft demand letters and pleadings that are distinct and aggressive — signaling clearly to the opposition that we are prepared to litigate, not just threaten. Most employers respond differently when they understand the other side is ready for trial.

Step Three
Outcome

We negotiate from a position of strength — not desperation. Whether that means a settlement that reflects what the case is worth or a trial that establishes accountability, the outcome we pursue is the one that actually serves your interests.

Are You a Medical Professional?

Healthcare employment disputes carry licensing consequences.

For physicians, CNMs, and licensed healthcare professionals, an employment dispute is never just an employment dispute. How it is handled can trigger consequences for your license, your NPDB record, and your hospital privileges that outlast the employment relationship itself.

This firm handles the employment claim and the licensing exposure simultaneously — because for healthcare professionals, they cannot be separated.
