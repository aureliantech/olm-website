Strategy Sessions & Fees

No. We believe that high-quality legal advice requires dedicated time and honest analysis. Our initial strategy sessions are paid where we review your situation in depth and provide actionable direction.

We do not view strategy sessions as sales calls. We view them as the first hour of legal work — and we treat them accordingly. You will leave with a clear understanding of your situation, your options, and what pursuing any of them would require.

A focused review of your specific situation — your facts, your documents, your timeline. Real legal analysis applied to what you actually have, not generic advice about what the law says in theory.

At the end, you will know: what your legal position is, what your realistic options are, what each option would require, and whether this firm is the right fit for your matter. Whether or not you retain us, that analysis is yours.

Fee structure depends on the matter. Most litigation and complex matters are handled on retainer, with the retainer amount set based on the scope and complexity of the case. Flat fees are available for defined-scope matters such as wills, powers of attorney, and contract review.

We discuss fees directly and without vagueness during or immediately after the strategy session. You will know exactly what representation would cost before you make any commitment.

You can book directly online at any time — no waiting for a call back. Use the booking link on the Strategy Session page, select a time that works, and the confirmation and payment instructions will come to you by email.

How the Firm Operates

Yes. This firm operates exclusively as a virtual practice. There is no physical office you need to visit. Strategy sessions are conducted remotely, documents are exchanged electronically, and all communication is handled by phone and email.

This is intentional — it eliminates overhead costs that would otherwise be passed to clients, and it allows the firm to serve clients across all of Texas and Arizona without geographic restriction.

Immediately. This firm does not have an intake queue, an onboarding process, or a waiting period before work begins. Once a retainer agreement is signed and the retainer is funded, the work starts.

For urgent matters — active board complaints, imminent hearings, situations where evidence needs to be preserved quickly — urgency is communicated at the consultation stage and addressed accordingly.

Your inquiry is treated with confidentiality from the first contact. Once attorney-client privilege is formally established through a retainer agreement, all communications are fully privileged.

Note: Please do not include sensitive, confidential, or incriminating details in initial web inquiries before privilege has been established. A phone call or the strategy session itself is the appropriate place for those details.

This firm is licensed in Texas and Arizona and serves clients across both states. The virtual model means your location within either state is not a limitation — we handle matters in Dallas, Houston, San Antonio, Phoenix, Tucson, and everywhere in between.

Some practice areas have more limited geographic scope — probate and estate planning services are primarily concentrated in the Rio Grande Valley (Hidalgo, Cameron, and Willacy County). Employment law, civil litigation, defamation, and business matters are handled statewide across Texas and Arizona.

Case Scenarios

Yes. This firm regularly accepts cases mid-stream from clients whose representation has stalled. The first step is a case assessment — we review what has been done, what has been missed, and what the current posture of the matter is.

If we can help and believe intervention will make a difference, we will tell you directly. If the situation has deteriorated to a point where options are limited, we will be honest about that too. Either way, you will have a clear picture after the assessment.

Before filing a lawsuit or making a formal demand, this firm conducts a review of the facts, the documents, and the legal theory to determine whether the claim is viable and whether the cost of pursuing it is justified by the likely recovery.

This is not a courtesy step — it is how we protect clients from investing time and money into matters that cannot be won, and how we ensure that when we do file, the case is built to survive dismissal and prevail at trial.
