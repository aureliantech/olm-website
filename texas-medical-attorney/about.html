Education & Early Career

A graduate of The University of Virginia and The Ohio State University Moritz College of Law, Octavia began her career in Phoenix, Arizona. She served as a bailiff and Superior Court law clerk before transitioning to active practice — giving her a judicial perspective on how cases are evaluated before she ever argued one.

As a felony prosecutor, she built evidentiary records from scratch — learning how institutional cases are constructed, how investigators prioritize their work, and how narratives harden into records that become very difficult to contest once they are complete. She carried that knowledge into complex civil litigation, including high-stakes medical malpractice defense where she first engaged directly with clinical records, expert witnesses, and the institutional dynamics of healthcare litigation.

The Move to Texas & Expansion into License Defense

Bringing this experience to the Rio Grande Valley, Octavia expanded her practice to meet the specific needs of South Texas — a region with a dense healthcare workforce, a complex border economy, and a client population that has historically had limited access to sophisticated legal representation.

Her work with physicians and licensed providers grew organically from her medical litigation background. Physicians facing board complaints were reaching out to general attorneys who had no framework for understanding what the TMB process actually involves — how it is structured, what investigators look for, how the record is used at the hearing stage, and what the consequences of a finding look like across a career. She stepped into that gap with a practice built specifically around the intersection of licensing risk, clinical records, and healthcare employment.

The Counter Protocol

The firm's signature approach — The Counter Protocol — is the direct product of this background. Most license defense attorneys wait for the board's process to unfold and then respond to it. That is a reactive posture that cedes narrative control to the investigators from day one.

The Counter Protocol treats a board complaint the way a prosecutor treats a criminal investigation: with an immediate parallel investigation that builds the defense record before the board's record is complete. Every piece of evidence that supports the provider's position is documented, preserved, and organized — so that when the board's process moves toward a hearing, the defense is not responding. It is presenting.

Core Principles

The principles that guide every case.

These are not marketing language. They are the standard against which every case and every decision is measured.

Truth
Evidence Over Narrative
Objective fact. Always.

We reject the idea of relative truth. Every defense strategy is built on documented facts — presented without varnish and without reliance on what the board would prefer to believe. If the facts support your position, we build the record that demonstrates it. If they do not, we are honest about that from the first conversation.

Courage
Standing in the Gap
A spine is not optional.

True advocacy for a physician under investigation requires the willingness to stand alone when the institutional pressure is significant. Board processes are designed to encourage resolution through consent agreements. We push back — through investigation, through advocacy, and through litigation where necessary — when the facts support a different outcome.

Righteous Advocacy
A Moral Duty to Push Back
Justice over convenience.

The regulatory process is too often used to discipline providers whose care was defensible, on the basis of complaints that do not reflect what actually happened. We view our role as a moral obligation to resist that — to ensure that the provider's record reflects reality, not the board's preferred narrative, and that careers are not destroyed by processes that were not conducted fairly.
