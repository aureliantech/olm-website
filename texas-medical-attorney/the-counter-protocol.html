The Core Principle

Most medical license defense attorneys are reactive by design. They wait to see what the board files. They read the complaint, draft a response, and begin preparing for a hearing they have already accepted as probable. By the time you retain them, they have already mentally conceded the first phase of your defense.

If you move first, you control the record. The board cannot dismiss what it hasn't investigated — but if we build an airtight factual counter-record and file an evidence-anchored rebuttal before the board's narrative hardens, dismissal becomes the path of least resistance.

The initial written response to the board is the single most critical document in your defense. A passive response — a form, a brief denial — signals to the board that they can continue building. An evidence-anchored counter-response forces a dismissal decision. Most attorneys never apply that pressure. The Counter Protocol does nothing else.

The Counter Protocol — Defined

Instead of waiting for the board to build its case, we build ours first — securing exculpatory evidence, drafting an evidence-backed rebuttal, and fighting for dismissal before a hearing is ever scheduled.

The Most Misunderstood Phase

Why the written rebuttal determines everything.

Most board complaints are not won at the hearing. They are won or lost in the written response phase — often within the first 60 days. A passive written response is a missed opportunity that cannot be recovered. If the board reads your response and sees no counter-evidence, no factual challenge, no legal pressure — they continue building.

They schedule the investigation. They retain their experts. By the time you get to a hearing, you are fighting a record that has been building for months while your side was waiting. The Counter Protocol refuses that dynamic. The rebuttal is built to force the board to make a decision — dismiss or defend its case against documented counter-evidence.
