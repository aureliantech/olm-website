The Rio Grande Valley's healthcare professionals serve one of the most underserved and complex patient populations in Texas.

McAllen physicians, midwives, nurse practitioners, and allied health professionals operate across a healthcare ecosystem anchored by DHR Health, South Texas Health System, and a network of federally qualified health centers and community health organizations serving a predominantly uninsured and underinsured population along the U.S.-Mexico border. Healthcare professionals in this region carry extraordinary professional burdens — and the Texas Medical Board holds them to the same standards as any other Texas licensee.

For RGV healthcare professionals, a TMB complaint triggers the same immediate consequences as anywhere in Texas: NPDB filing, hospital privilege review, DEA registration risk, and insurance panel disruption. For midwives practicing independently in a region where midwifery is deeply embedded in cultural and community healthcare traditions, a collaborative agreement termination can shut down an independent practice overnight — with fewer available replacement collaborators than in larger urban markets.
