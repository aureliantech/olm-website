There is no such thing as a minor board complaint. The board does not file investigations on minor concerns. By the time you receive notice, an investigative process has already begun.

That said, complaints that appear minor at filing are the ones most commonly dismissed quickly with an aggressive, evidence-anchored rebuttal — because the factual record needed to dismantle them is often straightforward. The Counter Protocol is most effective precisely in these situations, when the right response filed at the right time can close the matter before it escalates.
