Healthcare Employment Law · Texas

Your employment dispute
is also a licensing risk.
Most attorneys miss that.

For physicians, CNMs, and licensed healthcare professionals, an employment dispute is never just an employment dispute. How it resolves — and how it is handled — can trigger consequences for your license, your NPDB record, and your hospital privileges that outlast the employment relationship itself.

Important: Employment disputes involving healthcare professionals can trigger mandatory NPDB reporting, board complaints, and hospital privilege review — regardless of whether the employment claim has merit. Before you take any action, understand the full exposure.

What We Handle
Wrongful Termination from Hospital or Health System

Termination that violates an employment agreement, is retaliatory, or is based on discriminatory grounds. For physicians and healthcare professionals, the stakes are higher — a wrongful termination can trigger a hospital privilege review and NPDB reporting even before litigation begins.

Hospital Privilege Disputes & Peer Review

Challenges to hospital staff privileges — denial, suspension, or revocation — through peer review processes that are often used as employment weapons rather than legitimate quality assurance. Peer review decisions can be reported to the NPDB and trigger board scrutiny. We intervene at the peer review stage before the record is set.

Physician Non-Compete Review & Disputes

Physician non-competes in Texas are governed by specific statutory requirements including reasonable geographic scope and duration. Many are overbroad and unenforceable. Before you leave a practice or are told you cannot practice within a radius — get a professional review of what the agreement actually requires and what your real exposure is.

Whistleblower & Retaliation Protection

Healthcare professionals who report patient safety violations, billing fraud, HIPAA violations, or regulatory noncompliance are protected under multiple federal and state laws. Retaliation against a whistleblower — including termination, demotion, or privilege suspension — is actionable. We represent healthcare professionals who have faced adverse action for doing the right thing.

Employment Contract Review Before Signing

Physician employment agreements, group practice contracts, and hospital employment arrangements contain provisions that can affect your ability to practice for years after the relationship ends. Non-competes, non-solicitation clauses, tail coverage requirements, and termination provisions need professional review before you sign.

Why general employment attorneys miss the healthcare dimension.

A general employment attorney can handle the workplace law side of your dispute. What they often don't understand is the intersection with licensing boards, NPDB reporting obligations, peer review immunity doctrines, and the ways in which employment litigation strategy can inadvertently create licensing exposure. This firm handles both simultaneously — the employment claim and the licensing risk — because for healthcare professionals, they cannot be separated.

Facing a board complaint alongside an employment dispute?

Employment disputes and board complaints frequently travel together. A hospital system that terminates a physician often files or encourages a board complaint at the same time. If you are facing both, the strategy for each must account for the other. See our physician license defense practice →
