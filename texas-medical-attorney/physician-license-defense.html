What a TMB Complaint Triggers

A complaint does not wait for you to be ready.

The moment a complaint is filed, a sequence begins that does not pause while you decide whether to retain counsel. The board opens an investigation. Institutions that credential you begin receiving information. The record that will be used in any future proceeding starts being built — without your input.

Most physicians respond to this by calling a large firm that looks like it has resources. What they receive is a paralegal, an intake form, and a rebuttal filed weeks later by an associate who has never met them. That rebuttal becomes the foundation of their defense. If it is passive, the board treats it as passive — and continues building unopposed.

The initial written response to the board is the most consequential document in your entire defense. It signals whether there is a real fight here, or whether the board can proceed without meaningful resistance. That signal is sent once.
