What You're Actually Facing

What a Texas Medical Board Complaint Triggers Immediately

The initial written response to the board is the most consequential document in your defense. A passive response allows the board to continue building unopposed. An evidence-anchored counter-response forces a dismissal decision. That window opens once. Most attorneys miss it.

How The Counter Protocol Works →
NPDB Filing

Permanent record with the National Practitioner Data Bank. Every hospital, every credentialing body, every insurance panel checks it — indefinitely.

Hospital Privileges

Suspended or terminated during investigation. Insurance panel participation dropped. Your ability to practice your specialty is disrupted immediately.

DEA Registration

Revoked if the complaint involves prescribing. You cannot prescribe Schedule II–V controlled substances during the proceeding.

Hearing Timeline

Once you're in an administrative hearing, resolution averages 12 months. Your license, practice, and reputation remain in question for every month of that process.

Student Loan Debt

$200K–$300K in medical school debt does not disappear with license revocation. The debt survives. The income does not.

Communication

We communicate proactively. You do not follow up with us — we reach you first. If something changes in the investigation, you hear from us before you read about it anywhere else.

What We Need From You
Complete facts, not a curated presentation

Access to the relevant medical records and a full, unfiltered account of the facts as you know them. We ask the questions. You answer them completely. The strength of your defense depends on the accuracy of the record, not the presentation of it.
