What the Session Delivers

Exactly what you receive — no varnish.

One session. Real analysis. Clear direction. Whether or not you retain this firm afterward, what you learn in this session is yours.

01
An honest assessment of your legal position

Not what you want to hear — what the facts actually support. Where you are strong, where you are exposed, and what that means for how you should proceed.

02
Your realistic options — all of them

Every viable path forward, including doing nothing. The tradeoffs of each option explained clearly — what each costs, what each risks, and what each is likely to produce.

03
A frank economic assessment

If the cost of pursuing a matter outweighs the likely recovery, we will tell you. Directly. In the first session. You deserve to know that before you spend money on representation.

04
What immediate steps — if any — are required

Deadlines, evidence that needs to be preserved, communications that should stop immediately, filings that are time-sensitive. If there is something urgent, you will know before you leave.

05
Whether this firm is the right fit

If your matter falls outside our scope, we will tell you and refer you toward someone who handles it. We do not take cases we cannot win or represent clients whose interests we cannot serve effectively.

06
No obligation to retain

The session is a standalone service. Payment covers the analysis — not a commitment to further representation. If you decide to retain this firm, work begins immediately. If you do not, the analysis is still yours.

Why It's Paid

Free consultations are
sales calls.
This is not.

A free consultation is structurally a sales meeting — the attorney has not been paid to think carefully about your situation, and the incentive is to move you toward retention, not to give you honest analysis that might include "your case is not strong."

A paid consultation changes that dynamic entirely. The attorney's obligation is to you and to the accuracy of the analysis — not to whether you retain them. You get honest advice. The firm gets to work with clients who are genuinely suited to their practice.

This is not a policy designed to extract money. It is a policy designed to produce a better outcome for everyone in the room.

Who the Session Is For

You belong in this session if any of these describe you.

The session is designed for people at the beginning of a legal matter, in the middle of one that has stalled, or at a crossroads where they need honest direction before deciding how to proceed.

Something happened and you need to know where you stand

A termination, a breach, a false statement, a death in the family with a contested estate. You have facts. You need legal analysis applied to them.

You have an attorney but something is wrong

Your case has stalled, your attorney isn't communicating, a deadline may have been missed. You need an independent assessment of where things stand.

You need to decide whether to pursue legal action

You believe you have a claim but you're not sure it's worth pursuing. The session tells you whether it is — and what it would realistically cost and produce.

You have a document that needs review before you sign

An employment agreement, a non-compete, a settlement offer, a contract. You need to understand what you're agreeing to and whether there's room to negotiate.
