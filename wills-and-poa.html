The cost of doing nothing
If you pass away without a Will in Texas, the state decides who inherits your property — not you.

This process is often rigid, expensive, and rarely aligns with what the family actually needs. Worse, a lack of clear directives regarding your medical care or financial authority can leave your loved ones powerless during a crisis. The documents we draft speak for you when you cannot speak for yourself.

The most expensive litigation happens when a Will is vague. We approach every document with a litigator's eye — closing the loopholes before a challenger can find them.

Documents We Draft

Four documents. Complete protection.

Each document serves a distinct purpose. Together they ensure your property, your health, and your finances are handled according to your actual wishes — regardless of what happens.

01
Last Will & Testament

The cornerstone of your estate plan. Designates exactly who receives your property, who will care for your minor children, and who will serve as executor to manage the administration process. We draft wills designed to withstand scrutiny and minimize the risk of future contests — including specific provisions to avoid fractionalizing family land or assets.

Why it matters: Without one, Texas intestacy law controls distribution — often splitting property among heirs in ways that fracture family land, disrupt businesses, and contradict what you actually wanted.
02
Statutory Durable Power of Attorney

Authorizes a trusted person to manage your financial affairs if you become incapacitated. Bills still need to be paid and business must continue — this document ensures your agent can act immediately, preventing your assets from being frozen while a court process plays out.

Why "durable" matters: A standard POA terminates the moment you lose capacity — exactly when you need it most. The durable designation keeps it effective through incapacity.
03
Medical Power of Attorney

Designates a specific person to make medical decisions on your behalf if you are unable to communicate. This ensures your doctors take instructions from someone who understands your values — not a court-appointed guardian who does not know you.

Why it matters: Texas law has specific execution requirements for this document. A form that does not meet them will be rejected by hospitals and providers at the moment it is needed most.
04
Directive to Physicians (Living Will)

Documents your end-of-life care preferences — including decisions about life-sustaining treatment, artificial nutrition, and comfort care. This document relieves your family of having to make those decisions without guidance from you, during one of the most difficult moments they will face.

Why it matters: Without clear written directives, your family may be forced to make irreversible medical decisions under pressure, without knowing what you would have wanted.

Common Questions

What people ask before getting started.

If your question is not answered here, the strategy session will address it directly.

Do I need a will if I don't have many assets?

Yes. A will is not just about money — it names a guardian for your minor children, designates an executor to manage the process, and ensures your wishes are documented. Without one, these decisions default to Texas law and the courts, regardless of the size of the estate.

What happens to family land if there is no will in Texas?

Texas intestacy law divides it among your heirs — which can mean fractional ownership among multiple family members who may disagree about what to do with it. This is one of the most common sources of estate disputes in the Rio Grande Valley. A properly structured will prevents it.

Can I use an online will service instead?

You can, but online documents are generic by design. They cannot account for your specific family structure, your assets, Texas-specific execution requirements, or the language needed to prevent future contests. A document that looks complete can still fail if it was not properly witnessed or executed — and you will not find out until it is too late to fix it.

How long does the process take?

Most Wills & POA matters are completed within one to two weeks of the initial strategy session. The consultation covers your situation, your assets, and your wishes. We draft, you review, and we oversee the execution. The process is straightforward — but the documents that result from it are not generic.
